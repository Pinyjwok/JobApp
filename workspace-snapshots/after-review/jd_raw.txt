ABOUT LITTLE SPROUTS

Charming Cherubs Early Learning Centre is a distinguished, boutique provider in the heart of Mulgrave, offering a deeply personalized alternative to large-scale childcare. Operating as a community-focused service licensed for just 40 places, our center is designed to foster the kind of intimate, trusting relationships that only a small-scale environment can provide. Our educational philosophy is rooted in a nature-inspired, Reggio Emilia-influenced approach, viewing every child as a competent and capable researcher of their own world. With a prestigious "Exceeding" NQS rating across all seven quality areas, we pride ourselves on a high-touch service where our expansive outdoor nature gardens and mud kitchens aren't just play spaces—they are the central pillars of our intentional pedagogy. As a Lead Educator here, you aren't just managing a room; you are curating a high-quality, "home-like" sanctuary where excellence is embedded in every interaction.

POSITION PURPOSE

The Early Childhood Educator works as part of a collaborative team to provide high-quality education and care to children aged 0-5 years. Under the guidance of the Educational Leader, the Educator implements the educational program, documents children's learning, and supports children's wellbeing, health, safety, and development in accordance with the National Quality Framework (NQF).

This role requires genuine warmth, patience, creativity, and a commitment to building positive partnerships with families and the broader community.

KEY RESPONSIBILITIES

Program Implementation and Documentation
- Implement the educational program as designed by the Educational Leader, incorporating the Early Years Learning Framework (EYLF) and the Victorian Early Years Learning and Development Framework (VEYLDF)
- Observe children's interests, strengths, and developmental progress and contribute observations to the program planning cycle
- Document children's learning through learning stories, photographs, and developmental summaries using the Storypark digital platform
- Set up engaging, developmentally appropriate learning environments (indoors and outdoors) that reflect children's interests and promote inquiry-based play
- Contribute to the planning and delivery of intentional teaching experiences across all five EYLF Learning Outcomes

Children's Wellbeing, Health, and Safety
- Ensure the physical and emotional wellbeing of all children in your care at all times
- Supervise children actively and maintain required educator-to-child ratios as per the Education and Care Services National Regulations 2011
- Administer first aid as required and manage health-related incidents including medication administration, illness, and injury in accordance with centre policies
- Follow safe sleep practices for infants and maintain accurate sleep check records
- Support children's nutrition by assisting with mealtimes, respecting individual dietary requirements, and promoting healthy eating habits
- Implement behaviour guidance strategies that are positive, consistent, and aligned with the centre's behaviour guidance policy

Family and Community Partnerships
- Build warm, respectful, and professional relationships with families
- Communicate with families about their child's day, learning, and development through daily verbal updates, Storypark posts, and formal parent-teacher meetings
- Respect and respond to the diverse cultural, linguistic, and family backgrounds of enrolled children
- Support the transition of new children into the centre with sensitivity and care

Compliance and Professional Conduct
- Comply with all relevant legislation including the Education and Care Services National Law Act 2010, Education and Care Services National Regulations 2011, and the Children's Services Act 1996 (Vic)
- Adhere to the centre's Child Safe Standards policies and procedures
- Maintain the physical environment to meet licensing and accreditation standards, including cleaning, tidying, and safety checks
- Participate in team meetings, professional development sessions, and staff planning days as required
- Maintain confidentiality regarding children, families, and centre operations

SELECTION CRITERIA

Essential

2. Current Working with Children Check (Employee status) — Victorian
3. Current qualifications in First Aid (HLTAID012), CPR (HLTAID009), Asthma and Anaphylaxis management
4. An understanding of the National Quality Framework (NQF), including the National Quality Standard (NQS), the Early Years Learning Framework (EYLF), and the Education and Care Services National Regulations 2011
5. Experience working with children aged 0-5 years in a licensed early childhood education and care service (student placements will be considered)
6. A warm, nurturing, and patient approach to working with young children
7. Good written and verbal communication skills, including the ability to engage positively with families from diverse backgrounds
8. Ability to work collaboratively as part of a small, close-knit team

Desirable
9. Diploma of Early Childhood Education and Care (or currently studying towards)
10. Experience with digital documentation platforms such as Storypark, Kinderloop, or Xplor
11. Knowledge of the Reggio Emilia educational philosophy
12. An interest in nature-based or outdoor learning approaches
13. Current Victorian Driver's Licence
