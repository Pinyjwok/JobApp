## CV Format Recommendations

I've analyzed your writing style from your current CV and the recent tone analysis. I've noted that while your writing is warm and professional, there are several formatting standards used in modern professional CVs that will help your application stand out to hiring managers and pass through Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) more effectively.

**Current Style Patterns Identified:**
* **Mixed Perspective:** You currently alternate between "I" statements ("I am looking for...") and descriptive phrases.
* **Full Sentences:** Many points are written as complete sentences with periods.
* **Descriptive Focus:** Your responsibilities are clear, but could be more punchy to emphasize your impact.

**Proposed Professional CV Standards:**
* **Implicit First-Person** — We'll remove "I" and "Me" (e.g., "**Coordinated** daily nursery activities" instead of "I looked after the babies").
* **Telegraphic Bullets** — Using an "Action Verb + Object" structure without ending periods. This makes the CV much easier to scan in the 6 seconds a recruiter typically spends on it.
* **Bold Key Achievements** — We will highlight specific successes and metrics (e.g., "**Recipient of 'Employee of the Month' (March 2023)** for independently designing and implementing an Easter educational display").
* **Consistent Parallel Structure** — Every bullet point will start with a strong, active verb in the same tense.

**Why these changes?**
* **Readability:** Clear bullet points allow managers to find your credentials quickly.
* **Professionalism:** This is the standard "language" of Australian CVs in the Education sector.
* **Impact:** Shifting to action verbs (Coordinated, Facilitated, Maintained) makes your experience sound more active and expert.
